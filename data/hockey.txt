Field Hockey is a team sport played between two teams of eleven players using curved sticks to hit a ball into the opponent's goal.

The sport is governed internationally by the International Hockey Federation (FIH).

A standard match consists of four quarters of 15 minutes each.

Each team has ten field players and one goalkeeper.

Players score by hitting or pushing the ball into the goal from inside the shooting circle.

Penalty corners and penalty strokes are important scoring opportunities.

The Hockey World Cup and the Olympic Games are the biggest international tournaments.

India has a rich hockey history and has won multiple Olympic gold medals. Other strong nations include Australia, Netherlands, Germany, Belgium, Argentina, and England.

The stick has one flat side, and players may only use that side to play the ball.

Modern field hockey is played on synthetic turf, allowing faster gameplay.
