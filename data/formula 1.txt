Formula 1 (F1) is the highest class of international single-seater auto racing, governed by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). It is considered the pinnacle of motorsport and consists of a series of races called Grands Prix held around the world.

The Formula One World Championship began in 1950. Drivers compete individually for the Drivers' Championship, while teams compete for the Constructors' Championship.

Each Formula 1 team builds its own car within FIA technical regulations. Every team fields two drivers during a season.

A Formula 1 race weekend usually consists of practice sessions, qualifying, and the Grand Prix race. Qualifying determines the starting grid, with the fastest driver earning Pole Position.

Points are awarded to the top ten finishers:
1st - 25
2nd - 18
3rd - 15
4th - 12
5th - 10
6th - 8
7th - 6
8th - 4
9th - 2
10th - 1

The fastest lap may earn one additional point if the driver finishes in the top ten.

Famous Formula 1 teams include Ferrari, Mercedes, McLaren, Red Bull Racing, Williams, Aston Martin, Alpine, Haas, Sauber, and Racing Bulls.

Some of the most successful drivers in Formula 1 history include Michael Schumacher, Lewis Hamilton, Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Sebastian Vettel, Max Verstappen, Fernando Alonso, and Niki Lauda.

The Monaco Grand Prix is one of the most prestigious races in Formula 1.

Modern Formula 1 cars are powered by hybrid turbocharged V6 engines and use advanced aerodynamics to generate downforce for high-speed cornering.

Safety is extremely important in Formula 1. The Halo cockpit protection system significantly improves driver safety.

Formula 1 races are held on permanent racing circuits as well as temporary street circuits in countries around the world.
