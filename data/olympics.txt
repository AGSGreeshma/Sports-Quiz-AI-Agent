The modern Olympic Games were founded by Pierre de Coubertin and first held in Athens, Greece, in 1896.
The Olympics are organized by the International Olympic Committee, based in Lausanne, Switzerland.
The Summer and Winter Olympic Games are each held every four years, traditionally staggered two years apart.
The Olympic rings symbolize the union of the five inhabited continents through sport.
Athletes are awarded gold, silver, and bronze medals for finishing first, second, and third in their events.
The ancient Olympic Games originated in Olympia, Greece, and were held in honor of the god Zeus.
Jesse Owens won four gold medals in track and field at the 1936 Berlin Olympics.
Michael Phelps is the most decorated Olympian in history, having won numerous gold medals in swimming.
Usain Bolt won multiple Olympic gold medals in sprinting and set world records in the 100 and 200 meters.
The Olympic motto is "Citius, Altius, Fortius," meaning "Faster, Higher, Stronger."
The Olympic flame is lit in Olympia, Greece, and carried by torch relay to the host city before each Games.
Gymnastics, athletics, swimming, and cycling are among the foundational sports of the modern Olympic program.
The Paralympic Games are held shortly after the Olympic Games and feature elite athletes with disabilities.
Nadia Comaneci became the first gymnast to score a perfect 10 at the Olympics, achieving it at the 1976 Montreal Games.
The Winter Olympics feature sports such as skiing, ice skating, ice hockey, and bobsledding.
The Olympic host city is selected years in advance through a bidding process overseen by the International Olympic Committee.
Simone Biles is recognized as one of the most decorated gymnasts in Olympic and World Championship history.
The Olympic Games were cancelled during both World War I and World War II due to global conflict.
Women were first allowed to compete in the Olympic Games at the 1900 Paris Games.
The opening ceremony traditionally features a parade of nations, with Greece entering first and the host nation entering last.
