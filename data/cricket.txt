Cricket is played between two teams of eleven players each, using a bat and ball.
A standard cricket pitch is 22 yards long, with a wicket of three stumps at each end.
The three main formats of international cricket are Test matches, One Day Internationals, and Twenty20.
A Test match can last up to five days and consists of two innings per team.
One Day International matches limit each team to 50 overs per innings.
Twenty20 matches limit each team to 20 overs per innings, making them the shortest format.
A batter can be dismissed in several ways, including bowled, caught, leg before wicket, run out, and stumped.
Scoring six runs without the ball touching the ground is called a six, and four runs along the ground is a four.
The Cricket World Cup is the sport's premier One Day International tournament, held every four years.
The Ashes is a historic Test cricket series played between England and Australia.
The Indian Premier League is a major Twenty20 franchise tournament held annually in India.
Sir Donald Bradman is widely regarded as the greatest batter in cricket history, with a Test batting average near 99.94.
Sachin Tendulkar holds the record for the most international runs and centuries scored by any batter.
Sir Vivian Richards and Sir Garfield Sobers are celebrated as two of the greatest all-round cricketers from the West Indies.
A century in cricket refers to a batter scoring 100 or more runs in a single innings.
A bowler taking five wickets in a single innings achieves what is known as a five-wicket haul.
Cricket originated in England and is believed to date back to the 16th century.
The International Cricket Council is the global governing body for the sport of cricket.
A hat-trick in cricket occurs when a bowler dismisses three batters with three consecutive deliveries.
The Duckworth-Lewis-Stern method is used to recalculate target scores in limited-overs matches interrupted by weather.
