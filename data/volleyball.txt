Volleyball is a team sport played between two teams of six players separated by a net. The objective is to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's court while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in the United States.

A volleyball match is played as the best of five sets. The first four sets are played to 25 points, while the fifth set is played to 15 points. A team must win by at least two points.

Each team is allowed a maximum of three touches before returning the ball over the net.

Common skills include serving, passing, setting, spiking, blocking, and digging.

Players rotate clockwise whenever their team wins the right to serve after receiving.

The Libero is a defensive specialist who wears a different colored jersey and cannot attack the ball above net height.

Major international competitions include the Olympic Games, FIVB Volleyball World Championship, Volleyball Nations League, and Volleyball World Cup.

Some of the strongest volleyball nations include Brazil, Italy, Poland, Japan, the United States, Serbia, and France.

Indoor volleyball is played on a hard court, while beach volleyball is played on sand with teams of two players.
