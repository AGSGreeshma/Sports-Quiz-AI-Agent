Swimming is both an individual and team sport in which competitors race through water using different strokes.

Competitive swimming is governed by World Aquatics (formerly FINA).

The four primary swimming strokes are:
Freestyle
Backstroke
Breaststroke
Butterfly

Freestyle events are generally swum using the front crawl technique because it is the fastest stroke.

Olympic swimming events include individual races, relay races, and medley competitions.

A medley consists of Butterfly, Backstroke, Breaststroke, and Freestyle.

Swimmers start from starting blocks except in backstroke events, where they begin in the water.

World-class swimmers include Michael Phelps, Katie Ledecky, Ian Thorpe, Mark Spitz, Sarah Sjöström, and Caeleb Dressel.

Michael Phelps is the most decorated Olympian in history with 28 Olympic medals.

Swimming improves cardiovascular fitness, endurance, flexibility, and overall health.
