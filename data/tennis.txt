Tennis is played either as a singles match between two players or as a doubles match between two pairs.
A tennis match is won by taking a majority of sets, with players scoring points, games, and sets in sequence.
Points in a standard tennis game are scored as 15, 30, 40, and game, with a tie at 40-40 called deuce.
A player or team must win a set by at least two games, typically reaching six games first.
A tiebreak is commonly played when a set reaches six games apiece to decide the set winner.
The four Grand Slam tournaments are the Australian Open, the French Open, Wimbledon, and the US Open.
Wimbledon is the oldest tennis tournament in the world and is traditionally played on grass courts.
The French Open is played on clay courts at the Roland Garros complex in Paris.
The Australian Open and US Open are both played on hard courts.
Winning all four Grand Slam tournaments in a single calendar year is known as a Calendar Slam.
Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic are widely regarded among the greatest men's players in tennis history.
Serena Williams and Steffi Graf are celebrated among the most successful women's players in tennis history.
An ace occurs when a serve is untouched by the receiver and lands in for a point.
A let is called when a serve touches the net but still lands within the correct service box, and the serve is retaken.
Tennis balls must clear the net and land within the boundaries of the opponent's court to remain in play.
The Davis Cup is the premier international men's team tennis competition, held annually between nations.
The Fed Cup, now known as the Billie Jean King Cup, is the leading international women's team tennis competition.
A love score in tennis means zero points have been won in that game.
Tennis has been part of the Summer Olympic Games program since it was reintroduced in 1988.
A break of serve occurs when the receiving player wins a game against the player who was serving.
