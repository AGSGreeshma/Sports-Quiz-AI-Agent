Basketball is played between two teams of five players each on a rectangular court.
The objective of basketball is to score points by shooting the ball through the opposing team's hoop.
A standard basketball hoop is mounted 10 feet above the court surface.
Field goals are worth two points, shots made beyond the three-point line are worth three points, and free throws are worth one point.
The National Basketball Association, or NBA, is the premier professional basketball league in the United States.
An NBA game consists of four quarters, each 12 minutes long.
The FIBA Basketball World Cup is the sport's premier international tournament, organized by the International Basketball Federation.
Basketball was invented in 1891 by Canadian physical education instructor James Naismith.
A shot clock requires the offensive team to attempt a shot within a limited time, typically 24 seconds in the NBA.
Michael Jordan won six NBA championships with the Chicago Bulls and is widely regarded as one of the greatest players ever.
Kareem Abdul-Jabbar was known for his signature skyhook shot and a long, highly successful NBA career.
LeBron James is recognized as one of the most versatile and enduring players in basketball history.
A dunk occurs when a player forcefully scores by putting the ball directly through the hoop with one or both hands.
Dribbling is the technique of bouncing the ball while moving to advance it up the court legally.
A double dribble is a violation that occurs when a player dribbles with both hands simultaneously or stops and restarts dribbling.
The Olympic basketball tournament has been part of the Summer Games program since 1936.
A triple-double refers to a player recording double-digit totals in three statistical categories in a single game, such as points, rebounds, and assists.
Traveling is a violation that occurs when a player holding the ball moves their feet illegally without dribbling.
Basketball courts used in the NBA measure 94 feet long and 50 feet wide.
The WNBA is the premier professional basketball league for women in the United States, founded in 1996.
